The Parish of Horsley is in the Stroud District of Gloucestershire. The parish is a dispersed settlement in the Cotswold AONB and includes 16 small hamlets over an area of almost 6 square miles. It consists of some 300 properties and has a population of around 750. Horsley Village has a church, a primary school, a pub, a village hall, a pavilion (which houses the community shop) and a playing field and play area.

A Rich History

The settlement of Horsley can be dated back to 1066, when lands were granted to Troarn Abbey (Calvados, France). At this time it consisted (amongst other things) of a Mill, 6 plough lands, a prior, a monk, a chaplain (the three of whom founded 'Horsley Priory') and a knight (on horseback). An Active Rural Community

Horsley has a diverse, vibrant and thriving community, which is evident from the many volunteer-lead projects that have been successfully completed over the years. e.g.:

Horsley Playground Project

This was a community initiative to improve the children's playground, which was minimalist in design, occasionally flooded and 80% broken. Thanks to a grant from the Landfill Communities Fund, we were able to build this fantastic, landscaped play area that now draws families from all over the area.

Horsley Playground Project

Horsley '3in1' Project

This was a community initiative to refurbish St. Martin's church so that it could become a multi-use premises, to be used by the school during the week (as a main hall), as a venue for both public and private events, and to host the full range of church services.

Horsley Community Shop

When the local shop was due to close (fyi: the shop-keeping couple wanted to retire and needed their front room back), Horsley rallied to form a new co-operative community shop, staffed by volunteers and housed in a parish-owned pavilion building (btw: this was another community project, managed by the parish council and built mainly by local volunteers).

Horsley Neighbourhood Plan

The first release of Horsley's Local Neighbourhood Plan has just been published (September 2018), and is now open for public consultation. This is an accumulation of years of work by many volunteers, with a disparate range of skills and experience. It's a co-ordinated effort on behalf of the community to influence future development in this amazing area of outstanding natural beauty (AONB).

Many thanks to the Gloucestershire Environment Trust for their support.
